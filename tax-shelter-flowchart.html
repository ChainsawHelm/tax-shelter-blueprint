01

Foundation

Do these first — available to every W-2 employee

🏦 Max Out Employer 401(k)
Contribute the maximum: $23,500/yr (or $31,000 if age 50+).
Every dollar is pre-tax — directly reduces your taxable income. If your employer matches, this is free money.
🏥 Open & Max Your HSA TRIPLE TAX-FREE
$4,300 individual / $8,550 family.
The single best tax vehicle in the US tax code: deductible going in, grows tax-free, withdrawals tax-free for medical. After age 65, works like a traditional IRA for any spending.
Requires a High Deductible Health Plan (HDHP).
📈 Max Traditional or Roth IRA
$7,000/yr (or $8,000 if age 50+).
Traditional = tax deduction now, pay taxes later. Roth = pay taxes now, withdrawals are tax-free forever. Choose based on whether you expect higher or lower taxes in retirement.

02

Start a Business

A side business unlocks deductions your W-2 job can't touch

🚀 Launch a Legitimate Side Business
Freelancing, consulting, e-commerce, content creation — anything with genuine profit motive. You do NOT need to quit your day job. The IRS safe harbor: profitable in 3 of 5 years.
📄 Form a Single-Member LLC RECOMMENDED
An LLC provides liability protection (separates your personal assets from business debts). Tax-wise, a single-member LLC is "disregarded" — it files on Schedule C, same as a sole proprietorship. Simple setup, typically $50–500 depending on your state.
✂️ Deduct Business Expenses
Everything ordinary and necessary for your business becomes a deduction:
🏠 Home office
🚗 Vehicle (biz %)
💻 Equipment §179
🌐 Internet/phone
🛫 Business travel
📚 Education
🔧 Software/tools
🏥 Health ins.*
* Self-employed health insurance deduction if not covered by spouse's employer plan.
🏦 Open a Solo 401(k) BIG UNLOCK
As both employer and employee, you can shelter up to $69,000/yr. This is on top of your W-2 employer's 401(k). The employee portion is shared across plans, but the employer contribution (25% of net SE income) is separate and unlimited up to the cap.
?
Is your side income consistently above $50k/yr?
YES → Elect S-Corp (see below)
NO → Stay as LLC / Schedule C
S-Corp Election — Form 2553
Pay yourself a "reasonable salary" (e.g., $50,000). This is subject to payroll taxes (FICA 15.3%).
Take remaining profit as distributions — distributions are NOT subject to self-employment tax (15.3%). This is the key savings.
Requires running payroll (use Gusto, QuickBooks Payroll, etc.) and filing an S-Corp return (Form 1120-S).
Example Savings
$120k net income → $50k salary + $70k distribution
SE tax saved on $70k = ~$10,710/yr
👨‍👧‍👦 Hire Your Kids (Under 18)
Pay each child up to $14,600 (standard deduction) — they pay $0 income tax. If your business is a sole prop or single-member LLC (not S-Corp), you also avoid payroll taxes for kids under 18. Must be legitimate work at reasonable pay.
🌱 Fund Roth IRA for Your Kids
Once your child has earned income (from the job above), they can contribute to a Roth IRA. A teenager investing $7,000/yr in a Roth gets 50+ years of tax-free growth. This is generational wealth building.
03

Real Estate

The classic tax shelter — depreciation creates losses on paper while you cash-flow

🏠 Buy a Rental Property
Real estate is the most powerful legal tax shelter because of depreciation: the IRS lets you deduct the building's value over 27.5 years, even while the property appreciates in real market value. This is a "paper loss" — you deduct money you didn't actually spend.
Example — Depreciation
Buy a $300k property (land $60k, building $240k)
Annual depreciation: $240k ÷ 27.5 = $8,727/yr deduction — even while property gains value
✂️ Stack Rental Deductions
On top of depreciation, deduct: mortgage interest, repairs, insurance, property management fees, travel to the property, HOA fees, property taxes, advertising for tenants — all against rental income.
?
Can you or your spouse qualify as a Real Estate Professional? (750+ hrs/yr in RE activities)
YES → The Big Unlock (see below)
NO → Passive Loss Rules Apply
Real Estate Professional Status (REPS) vs. Passive Loss Rules
With REPS: Use ALL rental losses against your W-2 income. This is the "cheat code" — rental depreciation wipes out your taxable salary. Requires 750+ hours/yr AND more time in RE than any other job. One spouse qualifying is enough for MFJ returns.
Without REPS: Deduct up to $25,000 in rental losses against other income if AGI is under $100k. Phases out completely at $150k AGI. Unused losses carry forward.
🔄 1031 Exchange — Defer Gains Indefinitely
Sell a rental property → roll proceeds into a new property → defer all capital gains taxes. Repeat this your entire life. At death, your heirs receive a stepped-up cost basis — the accumulated gains are never taxed. This is how generational real estate wealth is built.
04

Advanced Strategies

Layer these on top once your foundation is solid

🏡 Augusta Rule §280A
Rent your personal home to your own business for up to 14 days/year. The rental income is tax-free to you (not reported). Your business deducts the rent as a legitimate expense. Must charge fair market rate and document with meeting minutes, comparable rental data, etc.
📊 Donate Appreciated Stock (Not Cash)
Instead of selling stock → paying capital gains → donating cash, donate the stock directly to charity. You deduct the full market value and never pay capital gains on the appreciation. Double benefit: bigger deduction + zero tax on gains.
🎁 Donor-Advised Fund (DAF) — Bunch Donations
If your annual giving is near the standard deduction threshold, bunch 3-5 years of donations into one year via a DAF. You get a massive deduction in year one (itemize), then take the standard deduction in subsequent years. Distribute from the DAF to charities over time.
📉 Tax-Loss Harvesting
Sell investments that are down to realize capital losses. Offset capital gains dollar-for-dollar, plus deduct up to $3,000/yr against ordinary income. Buy a similar (not "substantially identical") fund immediately to maintain exposure. Unused losses carry forward forever.
🎗️ Qualified Charitable Distribution AGE 70½+
Donate up to $105,000 directly from your IRA to charity. Counts toward your Required Minimum Distribution (RMD) but is NOT included in taxable income. Far better than withdrawing, paying tax, then donating.
⚠ Critical Warnings
✕ Profit motive required — the IRS expects your business to be profitable in 3 of 5 years. A fake business just for deductions is fraud.
✕ Don't over-deduct — claiming 100% business use of a personal vehicle or using your bedroom as a "home office" are audit red flags.
✕ Keep meticulous records — receipts, mileage logs, meeting minutes, time tracking. If you can't prove it, you can't deduct it.
✕ Spending ≠ saving — a $1,000 deduction in the 24% bracket saves $240. You're still out $760. Never spend just for the deduction.
✕ Consult a CPA — every situation is different. State taxes, AMT, phase-outs, and your specific income all change the math. Get professional advice.
