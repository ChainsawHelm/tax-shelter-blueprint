Foundation

Available to every W-2 employee — do these first
INCOME: Any
🏦 401(k) / 403(b) / 457(b)
Max contribution: $23,500/yr (2025). Catch-up if 50+: +$7,500 = $31,000. New "super catch-up" ages 60-63: +$11,250 = $34,750.

Every dollar is pre-tax — directly reduces your taxable income. If your employer matches, that's free money on top.

457(b) stacks — government employees can max BOTH a 403(b) and 457(b) = $47,000/yr sheltered.
Example
$100k salary, 24% bracket. Max 401(k) at $23,500 → save $5,640 in federal taxes. Employer match of 5% ($5,000) = $28,500 total sheltered.
🏥 Health Savings Account (HSA)
IRC §223 The single best tax vehicle in the US tax code. Triple tax-free:
1. Tax-deductible going in
2. Grows tax-free (invest it!)
3. Withdrawals tax-free for medical expenses

2025 limits: $4,300 individual / $8,550 family. Catch-up 55+: +$1,000.

After age 65: Withdrawals for ANY purpose are taxed as ordinary income (like a Traditional IRA) — but no penalty. Pay medical expenses out of pocket now, save receipts, reimburse yourself from HSA decades later for maximum tax-free compounding.
Example
Family HSA: $8,550/yr for 20 years at 8% return = $420,000+ tax-free for medical expenses in retirement.
📈 Traditional & Roth IRA
IRC §219 / §408A Limit: $7,000/yr ($8,000 if 50+).

Traditional IRA: Tax deduction now, pay taxes on withdrawal. Best if you expect lower taxes in retirement.
Roth IRA: No deduction now, but withdrawals are tax-free forever. Best if you expect higher taxes later.

Roth income limits (2026): Phase-out $153k-$168k single / $242k-$252k MFJ. But there's a workaround — see Tier 4 (Backdoor Roth).
🔄 Mega Backdoor Roth
IRC §402(c) Total 401(k) limit (employee + employer + after-tax): $72,000 (2026).

After maxing your regular 401(k) deferrals ($23,500) and counting employer match, you can contribute the remainder as after-tax contributions and immediately convert to Roth. That's up to ~$47,500 extra into Roth annually.

Requirement: Your employer's plan must allow after-tax contributions AND in-plan Roth conversions. Not all plans do — check with your plan administrator.
Total annual shelter
401(k): $23,500 + Mega Backdoor Roth: $47,500 + HSA: $8,550 + IRA: $7,000 = $86,550/yr sheltered

Start a Business

A side business unlocks deductions your W-2 job can't touch
INCOME: Any side income
📄 Form a Single-Member LLC
An LLC gives you liability protection (separates personal assets from business debts). Cost: $50-$500 depending on state.

Tax-wise, a single-member LLC is "disregarded" — files on Schedule C, same as a sole proprietorship. Simple and powerful.
✂️ Business Expense Deductions
Everything ordinary and necessary for your business reduces your taxable income:
🏠 Home office
🚗 Vehicle (biz %)
💻 Equipment §179
🌐 Internet / phone
🛫 Business travel
📚 Education / courses
🔧 Software / tools
🏥 Health insurance*

§179 Section 179 deduction: Now $2.5M limit (up from $1M per OBBBA), phaseout at $4M. Deduct the full cost of qualifying equipment in year 1 instead of depreciating over time.

Vehicle strategy: Vehicles over 6,000 lbs GVWR qualify for Section 179 up to $28,900 (SUV cap). Business use % must be documented.

*Self-employed health insurance deduction if not covered by spouse's employer plan.
🏦 Solo 401(k)
IRC §401(k) As both employer AND employee, you can contribute up to $69,000/yr.

Employee portion: $23,500 (shared with W-2 employer's plan).
Employer portion: 25% of net self-employment income (this is SEPARATE and stacks).

If you're also maxing a W-2 employer's 401(k), you can still make employer contributions to the Solo 401(k).
🏢 S-Corp Election — Save 15.3% SE Tax
Form 2553 When side income exceeds ~$50k/yr, elect S-Corp taxation:

1. Pay yourself a "reasonable salary" (subject to FICA 15.3%)
2. Take remaining profit as distributions — NOT subject to self-employment tax

Reasonable salary rules: The "60/40 rule" is a myth — the IRS uses market-based analysis. Document with comparable salary data. No mechanical formula.
Social Security wage base: $176,100 (2025).
S-Corp Savings Example
$120k net income → $50k salary + $70k distribution
SE tax saved on $70k: ~$10,710/yr
Politician Playbook
Joe Biden sheltered over $13 million through two S-corporations (CelticCapri Corp and Giacoppa Corp), avoiding the 3.8% Medicare surtax on net investment income.
📊 QBI Deduction — 20% Off Business Income
§199A Deduct 20% of qualified business income from pass-through entities (S-Corps, LLCs, sole props). Now permanent per OBBBA.

2026 phase-out: Starts at ~$200k single / $400k MFJ. Full phase-out at $275k / $550k.

Above phase-out: limited to greater of 50% of W-2 wages paid OR 25% of wages + 2.5% of property basis.
Example
$200k business income × 20% = $40,000 deduction. At 32% bracket = $12,800 saved.
🏡 Augusta Rule
§280A(g) Rent your personal home to your own business for up to 14 days/year.

You: The rental income is completely tax-free (not reported).
Your business: Deducts the rent as an ordinary business expense.

Documentation required: Written rental agreement, meeting agenda, attendee list, minutes, fair market value support (comparable rental rates), payment records, day-count tracker.

Must charge fair market rate. If comparable homes rent for $200/night, don't charge $1,000.
Example
14 days × $500/day (FMV) = $7,000 tax-free to you, $7,000 deduction for your business.
👨‍👧‍👦 Hire Your Kids (Under 18)
Pay each child up to $14,600 (standard deduction) — they pay $0 income tax.

If your business is a sole prop or single-member LLC (NOT S-Corp): also no payroll taxes for kids under 18. FICA §3121(b)(3)(A)

Must be legitimate work at reasonable pay — filing, cleaning, social media, etc.

Then fund a Roth IRA for them with their earned income. A teenager investing $7,000/yr gets 50+ years of tax-free compound growth.

Real Estate

The classic tax shelter — depreciation creates losses on paper while you cash-flow
INCOME: $75k+ recommended
🏠 Depreciation — The Paper Loss
§168 The IRS lets you deduct the building's value over 27.5 years (residential) or 39 years (commercial), even while the property appreciates in real market value. This is a "paper loss" — you deduct money you didn't spend.
Example
Buy $300k property (land $60k, building $240k).
Annual depreciation: $240k ÷ 27.5 = $8,727/yr deduction — for 27.5 years. Even while the property gains value.
⚡ Cost Segregation + Bonus Depreciation
A cost segregation study reclassifies 25-30% of a property into 5, 7, or 15-year assets (appliances, flooring, landscaping, etc.) instead of 27.5 years.

100% bonus depreciation RESTORED by OBBBA for assets acquired after Jan 19, 2025. The previous phase-down (80% → 60% → 40% → 20% → 0%) has been eliminated.

Combined: massive year-1 deductions.
Example
$500k property. Cost seg reclassifies 28% = $140k into short-life assets.
With 100% bonus depreciation: $140,000 first-year deduction (instead of ~$5,000/yr over 27.5 years).
🏨 Short-Term Rental (STR) Loophole
IRC §469 This is NOT a gray area — it's built into the tax code.

How it works: Rental properties with average guest stays of 7 days or fewer are classified as non-passive when the owner materially participates (100+ hours/year).

This means: STR losses (including accelerated depreciation from cost seg) can offset your W-2 income — WITHOUT needing Real Estate Professional Status.

Combined with cost segregation: Buy an Airbnb → cost seg study → 100% bonus depreciation → $100k-$150k+ write-off against your salary in year 1.
IRS Scrutiny
Must genuinely materially participate. Keep time logs. Average guest stay must be documented as ≤7 days.
🏗️ REPS — Real Estate Professional Status
§469(c)(7) If you or your spouse spend 750+ hours/year in real estate activities AND more time in RE than any other job:

ALL rental losses offset your W-2 income. This is the "cheat code" for high-earning couples — one spouse qualifies as REPS while the other keeps the day job.

Without REPS: You can still deduct up to $25,000 in rental losses if AGI < $100k (phases out by $150k).
🔄 1031 Exchange — Defer Gains Forever
§1031 Sell a rental property → reinvest proceeds into a new rental → defer ALL capital gains taxes. No limit on how many times you can do this.

At death: Heirs receive a stepped-up cost basis. All accumulated gains are never taxed. This is how generational real estate wealth is built.

Rules: Must identify replacement property within 45 days. Must close within 180 days. Must be "like-kind" (any real estate for any real estate). Use a qualified intermediary.
🏙️ Opportunity Zones
§1400Z Invest capital gains into a Qualified Opportunity Fund within 180 days of the gain event:

1. Defer the original capital gain until 2026
2. Hold for 10+ years → pay ZERO tax on all new appreciation in the OZ investment

OBBBA extended the program with new designations and rural-focused incentives.

Example: Sell stock with $500k gain → invest in OZ fund → in 10 years the OZ investment doubles to $1M → the $500k of new gains is 100% tax-free.
🏡 Primary Residence Exclusion
§121 Exclude up to $250,000 single / $500,000 married of capital gains on the sale of your primary residence. Must have lived there 2 of the last 5 years. Can use this every 2 years.

Retirement Account Hacks

Supercharge retirement savings beyond the basics
INCOME: $100k+
🚪 Backdoor Roth IRA
For high earners above Roth income limits:

1. Contribute $7,000 to a Traditional IRA (non-deductible)
2. Immediately convert to Roth IRA
3. No income limit on conversions

Pro-rata rule warning: If you have existing pre-tax IRA balances, a portion of the conversion will be taxable. Solution: roll pre-tax IRA money into your employer's 401(k) first.
🏛️ Defined Benefit / Cash Balance Plan
The most powerful retirement shelter for business owners. Annual contributions range from $100,000 to $400,000+ depending on age.

2026 limits: Annual benefit up to $290,000/yr in retirement. Lifetime contributions up to ~$3.7M. Compensation cap: $360,000.

Stacks on top of your 401(k). A 55-year-old business owner can shelter $350k+/yr between 401(k) + cash balance plan.

Tax savings at the 37% bracket: $130,000+ per year.
Combined Strategy
401(k): $23,500 + Cash balance plan: $250,000 + HSA: $8,550 = $282,050 sheltered/yr
🔓 Self-Directed IRA (SDIRA)
Invest IRA funds in real estate, private equity, crypto, precious metals, private loans — not just stocks and bonds.

Checkbook IRA LLC: Your SDIRA owns 100% of an LLC. You manage the LLC's checkbook directly — no custodian approval delays. Write checks to buy assets instantly.

Restricted investments: Collectibles, life insurance, S-Corp stock.

UBIT warning: If the SDIRA invests with debt (mortgage), Unrelated Debt-Financed Income tax applies to the leveraged portion. Also applies to active business income. File Form 990-T.
🪜 Roth Conversion Ladder (FIRE Strategy)
For early retirees who want to access retirement funds before 59½:

1. Retire with large Traditional IRA/401(k) balance
2. Each year, convert a portion to Roth in low-income years (fill the 0%, 10%, 12% brackets)
3. Each conversion starts a 5-year clock
4. After 5 years, withdraw converted principal penalty-free at any age

Bridge strategy: Use taxable accounts, Roth contributions (always withdrawable), or cash to cover the first 5 years while the ladder builds.
Example
$1M in Traditional IRA. Convert $50k/yr in the 12% bracket = $6,000/yr in taxes instead of 24-32% later. After 5 years: $50k/yr accessible penalty-free. Effective tax rate: 12% instead of 24%+.
🦄 Peter Thiel's Roth Strategy
PayPal co-founder Peter Thiel bought 1.7 million shares of his startup at $0.001/share inside a Roth IRA. Total cost: $1,700.

Those shares grew into $5 billion — all inside a Roth IRA, meaning 100% tax-free.

How it worked: Buy QSBS (qualified small business stock) at founder-price inside a Roth. As the company grows, all gains compound tax-free forever.

Current status: Congress has introduced bills to cap Roth IRA balances, but none have passed yet.
ProPublica Investigation
Thiel's Roth held shares in multiple startups. From $1,700 to $5 billion. Zero taxes owed. Ever.

Advanced Business Structures

C-Corps, entity stacking, and six-figure tax elimination
INCOME: $200k+
🏗️ QSBS — $15 Million Tax-Free
§1202 C-Corporation stock held 5+ years: exclude up to $15 million in capital gains (OBBBA increased from $10M). Or 10× your adjusted basis — whichever is greater.

OBBBA changes (July 2025):
• Per-issuer cap raised to $15M (inflation-adjusted starting 2027)
• Gross asset threshold raised to $75M (was $50M)
• Tiered exclusion: 50% at 3yr, 75% at 4yr, 100% at 5yr

Stack with trusts: Each trust beneficiary can claim a separate $15M exclusion. Family of 4 + 2 trusts = $90M tax-free.
Example
Found a C-Corp, issue yourself stock at $1/share. 5 years later, sell for $10M. Pay $0 in capital gains tax. All of it.
🏢 C-Corp — 21% Flat Rate
When business profits exceed personal tax brackets (32-37%), a C-Corp's flat 21% rate becomes attractive.

Accumulated earnings: Retain and reinvest profits inside the C-Corp at 21%. Only pay personal tax when you take money out as salary or dividends.

Qualified dividends: When you do take money out, qualified dividends are taxed at 0%/15%/20% capital gains rates, not ordinary income rates.

Combined effective rate: 21% corporate + 15% dividend = ~32.85% total — comparable to the 32% personal bracket but with the ability to time when you take income.
🏗️ Entity Stacking
How wealthy business owners structure multiple entities:

Operating LLC → does the actual business work
Management LLC → charges management fees to the operating company
Holding LLC → owns assets (real estate, IP, equipment) and leases them to the operating entity

Each entity can be in a different state (Wyoming for privacy, Nevada for asset protection, South Dakota for trusts). Management fees shift income between entities to optimize tax treatment across jurisdictions.
🛡️ Captive Insurance
§831(b) Create your own insurance company. Your operating business pays premiums (deductible). The captive pays tax only on investment income, not on premiums received.

Premium limit: $2.85M/yr (2025, inflation-adjusted).

The captive can insure risks not covered by commercial insurers (cyber liability, supply chain disruption, reputation damage).
IRS Warning
As of January 2025, certain micro-captive transactions are listed transactions requiring enhanced disclosure. On the IRS "Dirty Dozen" list. Must have genuine risk transfer and reasonable premiums. Consult a captive insurance specialist.
💰 S-Corp + Cash Balance + 401(k) Combo
The ultimate business owner tax shelter stack:

1. S-Corp: Pay reasonable salary → save SE tax on distributions
2. Max 401(k): $23,500 employee + employer match
3. Cash Balance Plan: $200,000-$400,000+ additional
4. QBI Deduction: 20% off remaining business income
5. Business deductions: Home office, vehicle, equipment, etc.
Total Shelter — Age 55, $500k Business Income
401(k): $31,000 + Cash Balance: $300,000 + HSA: $8,550 + S-Corp distribution savings: $15,000 + QBI deduction: $30,000 + Business deductions: $40,000 = $424,550 sheltered or saved

Trusts & Estate Planning

Transfer wealth across generations with minimal tax erosion
NET WORTH: $1M+
🎯 GRAT — Grantor Retained Annuity Trust
§2702 Transfer appreciating assets to a trust. Receive annuity payments back over a set term (typically 2-10 years).

If assets grow faster than the IRS §7520 assumed interest rate, the excess passes to heirs gift and estate tax-free.

"Zeroed-out GRAT": Set the annuity equal to 100% of the transferred value = zero reportable taxable gift. If assets outperform the §7520 rate, pure upside to heirs.

Rolling GRATs: Use 2-year terms back-to-back. If one GRAT underperforms, you lose nothing. If it outperforms, the excess transfers tax-free. Heads you win, tails you break even.
Proposed Reform
Congress has proposed requiring minimum 10-year terms and 25% minimum remainder value. Not yet enacted.
🔧 IDGT — Intentionally Defective Grantor Trust
Rev. Rul. 85-13 An irrevocable trust that's "defective" on purpose: income is taxed to the grantor (you), but assets are excluded from your estate.

Benefits:
• You pay the trust's income taxes = additional tax-free gift to beneficiaries
• Trust assets grow without "tax drag"
• Installment sale to IDGT is NOT a taxable event

Estate freeze technique: Seed IDGT with 10% equity. Sell appreciated business/assets to the trust for a promissory note at AFR interest rate. All future appreciation transfers to beneficiaries estate-tax-free. You receive only the fixed note payments.
🏰 Dynasty Trust
Leverage the GST (Generation-Skipping Transfer) exemption to create a trust that lasts for hundreds of years — or forever.

Best states: Nevada, South Dakota, Alaska — no state income tax on trust income AND no rule against perpetuities (trusts can last forever).

Assets compound inside the trust across generations without estate tax at each death. A $15M gift today could become $100M+ for your grandchildren's grandchildren — all estate-tax-free.
🏢 Family Limited Partnership (FLP)
Transfer business or investment assets to an FLP, then gift limited partnership interests to family members.

Valuation discounts:
• Lack of control (minority interest): 15-40% discount
• Lack of marketability: 10-30% discount
• Combined: 25-60% discount on taxable value

Example: $2M in assets → FLP with 30% combined discount → taxable gift value = $1.4M. You saved $600,000 in taxable value (at 40% estate tax = $240,000 in tax savings).
IRS Scrutiny
FLPs must have a legitimate business purpose beyond tax savings. Overly aggressive discounts attract audits. Get a qualified appraisal.
💎 Estate & Gift Tax Exemption
OBBBA made the increased exemption permanent:

• Lifetime exemption: $15M per person / $30M married (2026, inflation-adjusted)
• Annual gift exclusion: $19,000/person (unlimited recipients)
• 529 superfunding: $95,000 in one year ($190k married) = 5 years of annual exclusions upfront
• Married couples can "split" gifts — effectively $38,000/year per recipient

Estate tax rate: 40% on amounts above the exemption.
💝 Charitable Trusts (CRT / CLT)
Charitable Remainder Trust (CRT/CRUT): §664
You get an income stream (5-50% of trust value annually). Remainder goes to charity. Partial income tax deduction upfront. Sell appreciated assets inside the CRT = no capital gains on the sale.

Charitable Lead Trust (CLT):
Reverse of CRT — charity gets income first, then remainder passes to your heirs. If trust growth exceeds the §7520 rate, excess transfers to heirs gift/estate tax-free.

ILIT (Irrevocable Life Insurance Trust):
Life insurance proceeds held outside your estate. Fund with annual gift exclusion via Crummey powers. $5M life insurance policy = $5M tax-free to heirs, not subject to estate tax.

The Billionaire Playbook

How the ultra-wealthy pay effective tax rates under 5%
NET WORTH: $10M+
💀 Buy, Borrow, Die
The three-step strategy that lets billionaires live lavishly while paying almost nothing in taxes:

1. BUY: Accumulate appreciating assets (stocks, real estate, art). Never sell. Unrealized gains are NOT taxed.

2. BORROW: Take out Securities-Backed Lines of Credit (SBLOCs) against your portfolio. Borrow 50-95% of asset value at low interest rates. Loan proceeds are NOT income — not taxable.

3. DIE: At death, heirs receive assets with a stepped-up cost basis. ALL accumulated capital gains are wiped out. Heirs can sell immediately and pay $0 in capital gains tax. Or they can start the cycle again.
ProPublica: The Secret IRS Files
The top 25 wealthiest Americans (2014-2018):
• Collective wealth grew by $401 billion
• Total federal income taxes paid: $13.6 billion
• True tax rate: 3.4%

Jeff Bezos: Wealth grew $127 billion. Paid $1.4B in taxes. True rate: 1.1%. Paid $0 in 2007 and 2011.
Warren Buffett: True rate: 0.1%. Reported income of $11.6-$25M while wealth grew by $24B.
Elon Musk: Paid $0 federal income tax in 2018.
🛡️ Private Placement Life Insurance (PPLI)
Put hedge fund, private equity, or alternative investments inside a life insurance wrapper. The investment grows tax-free forever.

• No income tax on investment gains inside the policy
• No capital gains tax — ever
• No estate tax if structured properly (ILIT)
• Access cash via policy loans (tax-free)

Minimums: Typically $1M+ initial premium. Offered by offshore carriers (Bermuda, Cayman Islands) and select US carriers.

This is how ultra-wealthy families put their hedge fund investments inside an insurance shell and never pay taxes on the returns.
🏛️ Private Foundation
Create your own charitable foundation. Income tax deduction on contributions: 30% of AGI for cash, 20% for appreciated assets.

What you control:
• Investment of all foundation assets (hire your own money managers)
• Hiring decisions (employ family members at "reasonable" salaries)
• Travel for "charitable purposes" (site visits, conferences, research)
• Grant-making decisions (which charities receive funding)

Must distribute: At least 5% of assets annually for charitable purposes. Administrative expenses count toward this 5%.

Reality: A private foundation lets wealthy families maintain control over assets, employ family, fund travel, and get tax deductions — all while supporting genuine charitable causes.
🛢️ Oil & Gas Investments
Why politicians invest in oil: The tax code gives oil and gas investments uniquely favorable treatment.

Intangible Drilling Costs (IDCs): 65-80% of well cost. 100% deductible in year 1 for independent producers. These aren't passive losses — they can offset W-2 income.

Working interest exception: §469 A working interest in oil/gas is NOT a passive activity regardless of your participation level. Losses offset active income (salary, business income).

Percentage depletion: 15% of gross income deducted from oil/gas production — even AFTER you've recovered your full investment. One of the few deductions that can exceed your basis.

Tangible Drilling Costs: Equipment (wellheads, casing) — 100% deductible via depreciation. 40% upfront in 2025.
Example
Invest $100k in an oil well. IDCs = $75k (deductible year 1). TDCs = $25k (depreciated). At 37% bracket: $27,750 tax savings in year 1 on a $100k investment. Plus ongoing depletion deductions.
🌲 Conservation Easements
Donate development rights on land to a land trust. Deduct the difference between the land's "highest and best use" value and its restricted value.

Historically: Syndicated deals offered 4:1 ratios — invest $250k, get $1M deduction.
IRS Crackdown
Syndicated conservation easements are now a listed transaction if the deduction exceeds 2.5× the investor's basis. 40% gross valuation misstatement penalty applies. SECURE 2.0 Act restricted partnership/S-Corp deductions.

Still legal: Genuine conservation easements on land you actually own. But the syndicated "invest and deduct" deals are effectively dead.
📝 Installment Sale to Grantor Trust
Sell appreciated assets to your own IDGT in exchange for a promissory note at the AFR (Applicable Federal Rate).

Why it works: Under Rev. Rul. 85-13, a sale to your own grantor trust is not a taxable event. No capital gains triggered.

Estate freeze: You swap an appreciating asset for a fixed-rate note. All future growth above the AFR interest rate transfers to trust beneficiaries estate-tax-free.

Structure: Seed IDGT with 10% equity (gift). Sell remaining 90% for a note. Trust pays you interest at AFR (currently very low). All upside flows to the next generation.

Geographic & Advanced Strategies

Location arbitrage, crypto, charitable optimization, and more
INCOME: Varies
🗺️ No-Income-Tax States
9 states with zero state income tax: Alaska, Florida, Nevada, New Hampshire, South Dakota, Tennessee, Texas, Washington, Wyoming.

A high earner at $200k saves $15,000+/yr moving from a high-tax state (CA 13.3%, NY 10.9%) to a zero-tax state.

Caveat: No-income-tax states often have higher property taxes (TX, NH) or sales taxes (TN, NV 9%+). Calculate total tax burden, not just income tax.
California → Florida
$500k income × 13.3% CA top rate = ~$55,000/yr saved by moving to FL.
🏝️ Puerto Rico Act 60
Corporate tax: 4% (vs. 21% federal). Capital gains: 0% for pre-2026 decree holders, 4% for new applicants after 2025.

Requirements: Become a bona fide PR resident (183+ days/yr). Must not have been a PR resident in 15 years prior.

Program extended through 2055. Dividends from PR-sourced business: 100% tax-exempt.

Critical deadline: Apply before end of 2025 to lock in the 0% capital gains rate.
🌐 State Trust Situs Planning
Create trusts in states with no state income tax on trust income AND no rule against perpetuities:

• South Dakota: No income tax, perpetual trusts, strong asset protection, trust-friendly courts
• Nevada: Same + no franchise tax, 365-year trust term
• Alaska: Same + domestic asset protection trusts (self-settled)

A California resident creating a dynasty trust in South Dakota avoids 13.3% state income tax on all trust income — legally.
₿ Cryptocurrency Tax Strategies
Wash sale rule does NOT apply to crypto (as of 2026). Crypto is classified as "property," not "securities." You can sell at a loss and immediately rebuy the same asset — harvesting the tax loss while maintaining your position.

• Tax-loss harvesting: Sell losers, offset gains + $3k/yr against ordinary income. Buy back immediately
• Self-directed IRA: Hold crypto in a Roth SDIRA = tax-free growth
• Mining: Equipment and electricity costs are deductible business expenses

Coming changes: Form 1099-DA broker reporting starts 2025 (gross proceeds) and 2026 (cost basis). Wash sale extension has appeared in draft bills but hasn't passed.
Legislation Watch
The PARITY Act proposes extending wash sale rules to crypto. Monitor closely — this window may close.
✈️ Foreign Earned Income Exclusion
§911 US citizens living abroad can exclude up to $126,500 (2025) of foreign earned income from federal taxes. Must meet either:

• Physical presence test: 330+ days outside the US in a 12-month period
• Bona fide residence test: Established residence in a foreign country for a full tax year

Plus the Foreign Housing Exclusion for qualifying housing expenses above a base amount.
🎁 Donor-Advised Fund (DAF) Bunching
Strategy: Concentrate 3-5 years of charitable donations into a single year via a DAF. Itemize that year (exceeding the standard deduction). Take the standard deduction in other years.

How it works: Contribute cash or appreciated stock to a DAF → immediate tax deduction → distribute to charities over time at your discretion.

2026 standard deduction: $16,100 single / $32,200 MFJ.

New for 2026: 0.5% AGI floor on charitable deductions (only amounts exceeding 0.5% of AGI are deductible). Makes bunching even more valuable.
Example
Give $5k/yr normally × 5 years = $25k. Below standard deduction each year = $0 tax benefit.
Bunch: Give $25k in year 1 via DAF → itemize → ~$5,000-$8,000 tax savings. Years 2-5: take standard deduction. Same giving, better tax outcome.
📉 Tax-Loss Harvesting
Sell investments that are down to realize capital losses. Offset capital gains dollar-for-dollar, plus deduct up to $3,000/yr against ordinary income. Unused losses carry forward forever.

Buy a similar (not "substantially identical") fund immediately to maintain market exposure. Example: sell S&P 500 index fund → buy total market fund.

Wash sale rule (30 days) applies to stocks and securities but NOT crypto (see above).
🎗️ Qualified Charitable Distribution (Age 70½+)
§408(d)(8) Donate up to $105,000 directly from your IRA to charity. Counts toward your Required Minimum Distribution (RMD) but is NOT included in taxable income.

Far better than: withdraw IRA → pay income tax → donate cash → claim deduction. QCD skips all of that — the money goes directly from IRA to charity, never hits your tax return.
